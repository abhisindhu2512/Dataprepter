Generative AI Developer Interview Question Set

Q1: What is Generative AI?
Answer:
  • Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence techniques that can create new content, such as images, text, music, or code, often mimicking human creativity.
  • These models learn the patterns and structure of the data and generate similar new data.
  • Examples: GPT (language generation), DALL-E (image synthesis).
Q2: What are the top applications of Generative AI?
Answer:
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Used for text generation, language translation, and conversational AI like chatbots.
  • Content Generation: Auto-generates articles, stories, poetry, and marketing copy for content creators.
  • Image & Video Synthesis: Generates images (DALL-E, Stable Diffusion), edits photos, and creates deepfakes.
  • Code Generation: Assists in writing software code (e.g., GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT Code Interpreter).
  • Drug Discovery & Design: Designs novel molecules and proteins using generative chemistry models.
  • Art & Music: Composes music, creates arts, and generates designs.
Q3: How does a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) work?
Answer:
  • A GAN consists of two neural networks: a generator and a discriminator.
  • The generator creates fake data (e.g., images) trying to look like real data.
  • The discriminator evaluates data and classifies it as real or fake.
  • They are trained together: the generator tries to fool the discriminator, and the discriminator tries not to be fooled.
  • Through this process, the generator improves until the fake data is indistinguishable from real data.
Q4: What is the Transformer architecture and why is it important for Generative AI?
Answer:
  • The Transformer is a deep learning model architecture based on self-attention mechanisms.
  • It enables efficient parallel training and can model long-range dependencies better than RNNs.
  • The Transformer is the foundational architecture for state-of-the-art language models (like GPT, BERT, T5).
  • It powers the most advanced text generation, translation, and understanding systems today.
Q5: Mention key ethical concerns of Generative AI.
Answer:
  • Misinformation: Generating realistic fake news, deepfakes, or forged images.
  • Bias & Fairness: Reinforcing or amplifying biases present in training data.
  • Copyright Infringement: Creating content that unintentionally copies original works.
  • Privacy: Revealing sensitive or private information from training data.
  • Malicious Use: Generating phishing emails, malware, or explicit content.
Q6: What metrics are used to evaluate the performance of a text-generating model?
Answer:
  • BLEU, ROUGE, METEOR: Compare similarity of generated text to reference text (often for translation or summarization).
  • Perplexity: Measures how well a model predicts a sample (lower is better).
  • BERTScore: Measures semantic similarity between generated and reference texts.
  • Human Evaluation: For fluency, coherence, creativity, factual accuracy, and relevance.
Q7: What is Diffusion Modeling in the context of Generative AI?
Answer:
  • Diffusion models are generative models that start with random noise and gradually denoise it to produce a desired output (like an image).
  • The model learns to reverse the process of adding noise, step-by-step, to synthesize realistic data.
  • State-of-the-art image generators (e.g., Stable Diffusion, Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models) use this approach for high-quality image synthesis.
