How to Answer 'Explain a Recent Project?'

This is one of the most common and crucial data science interview questions. Your answer is an excellent opportunity to showcase your technical expertise, problem-solving process, and the impact of your work.

1. Follow a Clear Structure (STAR Method)

  • S – Situation: Briefly introduce the context and the motivation behind the project.
  • T – Task: State your specific responsibility in the project.
  • A – Action: Explain the approaches, tools, and techniques you used. Highlight your coding, modeling, or data wrangling efforts.
  • R – Result: Conclude with a measurable outcome, business impact, or what you learned.

2. Showcase Your Skills and Problem-Solving

  • Technical Skills: Mention libraries, platforms, or models (e.g., Python, SQL, scikit-learn, neural networks).
  • Decisions & Challenges: Talk about how you approached key choices and any obstacles you solved.
  • Communication: If you worked with stakeholders or explained results, bring it up!

3. Emphasize Impact

  • Use numbers or business KPIs where possible ("increased accuracy by 12%", "saved 10 work-hours per week").
  • Mention lessons learned or how it prepared you for future work.

Example Response

"In my previous internship, I worked on predicting customer churn for a subscription service (Situation). My role was to clean the data and build a predictive model (Task). I applied feature engineering, handled missing data, and built a random forest using Python’s scikit-learn (Action). My model improved churn prediction accuracy by 14%, enabling the company to proactively reach out to at-risk customers (Result)."

4. Tips for Success

  • Keep your answer within 2-3 minutes.
  • Practice aloud and refine your story for clarity.
  • Be honest about any challenges or failures, and share what you learned!
